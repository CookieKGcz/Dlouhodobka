# Personal Data Protection
## Privacy protection vs Personal data protection
- *Privacy* = space for the development of unique identity, private opinion, dissent
- ICT - need for preemptive protection framework
- *Protection of personal data* - limitations for using personal data and measures for protection of the individual, when doing so
- GDPR
	- https://www.privacy-regulation.eu/cs/index.htm
	- https://gdprhub.eu/index.php?title=Welcome_to_GDPRhub
## Protected value - privacy in digital age
- standard legal framework for privacy protection was reactive = not enough for fast paced digital age
- Personal data protection = rules for data-based operation with possible risk/harms to individual
- "Forcing" the data subject  perspective into business structures
## Terminology
- Article 4 GDPR - Definitions
	- *personal data* = any information about a natural person (= data subject) by which he or she can be identified, directly or indirectly
	- *processing* = any structured or (semi)automated operation on personal data
- *controller* = the entity that initiates the processing of personal data
- *processor* = an entity that carries out processing of personal data at the initiative of another
- X *employee* = follows instructions of controller/processor
## Basic principles
- Article 5 GDPR - processing prinicples
- *Accountability* - the controller is responsible for compliance with this policy and must be able to demonstrate such compliance
## Lawfulness = purpose + scope + legal basis
- Article 6 GDPR - lawfulness of processing
	- formulation of the purpose + reasonable scope of the personal data concerned defined + legal basis for processing established
## Sensitive personal data + stricter regime
- Articles 9 and 10 GDPR
	- their misuse may be particularly serious for the person concerned
	- special processing regime = prohibition of processing, except
		- explicit consent
		- data manifestly disclosed by the data subject
		- necessary for the establishment, exercise or defense if legal claims
## Personal data controllers duties 101
- **SCOPE**
	- Clarify what may be personal data and get an idea of how and what standard or sensitive personal data is processed
- **PURPOSE**
	- Be able to determine why it is processed
- **BASIS**
	- Be able to determine whether it is processed for a legal obligation, performance of a contract or a particular interest.
- **TRANSPARENCY**
	- Include provisions within contracts relating to the handling, security and disposal of personal data.
- **PROPORTIONALITY**
	- Be generally aware and reflecting the principles for handling personal data *(transparency, proportionality of scope and duration of storage, security, accuracy, purpose limitation)*.
- **DOCUMENTATION**
	- Have a general overview of internal processes, have some form of records of what is processed, how and when *(overview of contracts, overview of activities, overview of internal structure or some other documents)*
- **TRAINING**
	- communicate to employees the basic rules and limitations when working with personal data
- **SECURITY**
	- Have an idea of the measures taken to secure the system and the personal data and justification for their adequacy *(server room with restricted access, computers with user accounts and passwords, network management with security features, cameras, staff training, backup policy, etc.)*
- **INCIDENT HANDLING**
	- Train employees on how to act and who to contact in case of data loss or corruption *(loss of company equipment / computer virus infection of the system)* and identify the person responsible for reporting to the regulatory authority *(admin/manager)*
- **CONTACT POINT**
	- Identify and publicize a point of contact for customers regarding questions or requests regarding their rights and train the person responsible for those requests on how to address those requests
## Transfer of personal data to non-EU countries
- Articles 44-50 GDPR
	- Within the EU, the principle of free movement of personal data applies
	- Transfers outside the EU require an adequate level of protection of the rights and freedoms of the data subjects
		- EC adequacy decision - EU-US Data Privacy Framework
		- Standard contractual clauses and data protection
		- Biding corp. rules ...
		- Explicit consent or necessity in specific situation
## Rights of the data subject
- Right to information - Article 13-14 GDPR - transparency
	- What data is processed, why, by whom, how was the data obtained etc.
- Right of access to data - A. 15 GDPR - accessibility
	- Info on request + provision of a free copy
- Right to rectification - A. 16 GDPR
	- Right to have inaccuracies completed/corrected without delay.
- Right to erasure (to be forgotten) - A. 17
- Right to object to processing - A. 21
- Notification of rectification, erasure or restriction of processing - A. 19
## Claims and course of action for the data subject
- Art. 77-82
- Data subjects may be represented by a non-profit body/organization or association
- Data subjects may lodge complaints with the supervisory authority
- Judicial protection of the data subject
- Direct claims for damages

- Office of Personal Data Protection: https://uoou.gov.cz/en

# Privacy techniques and selected issues
## Data aggregation
- In general this concerns merging or compiling data from more datasets with an intent to prepare and combined dataset for some data processing that may not have been possible prior to the merge
- Such a combination of data at a certain sensitivity level can yield information at a higher sensitivity level
## Inference
- Inference if information of higher sensitivity by processing  and analyzing information at a lower level of sensitivity
- or
- Indirect access to information without a direct access to data that represents this information
## Statistical databases
- contain sensitive information on individuals.
- Their use only for statistical queries to get understanding about the needs and features of society/citizens and formulation of government policies.
- Results of queries in such databases cannot provide information about particular individuals.
## Database compromise
- The result of query series is just 1 record
	- the database was "positively compromised"
- Following attempts to yield more information
	- The result is 0 or 1 records
	- Partial database compromise
- Partial compromise 
	- Information about an entity even without the precise value
# Computer ethics
## Ethics, c. e.
- Ethics
	- area of philosophy (moral philosophy) studying concepts of right and wrong behavior
		- Studies/proposes systems of moral principles and rules of conduct/behavior
- Computer ethics
	- application of systems of moral values and rules for work with computers
		- Changes in technology => changes in society and its behavior, values.
## Motivating behavior (Don Parker)
- Power of law and regulations (fear of punishment, rule if force)
- Power of conventions (professional codes, codes of conduct - voluntary or semi-voluntary)
- Moral settings (customs, experience of social circles)
