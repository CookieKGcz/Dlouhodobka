- quality or state where one is (a) not observed and (b) disturbed by other people.
- Wikipedia: ability of an individual or group to seclude (a) themselves or (b) information about themselves, and thereby express themselves selectively.

- Information privacy is concerned namely with the control of information, namely personal data and other sensitive information.
## Private information
- Such information where subjects generally mean they do not want the information shared with others or they personally would like to control the dissemination of this information, sharing it with some but not with others
## Personal data
- Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person
- The same data item can sometimes be shown to identify a person and other times not...
## Information privacy
- More a concept for informal motivation to provide protection of private data, rules for this protection and its controls, sharing with other subjects, etc.
### Anonymity
- property of a system that supports the ability of a user to use system resources or services without revealing the identity of this system user. Not protecting the subject (in a computer sys.)
	- Extremely rare - full anonymity can be guaranteed only for a particular completely isolated system with properly designed anonymity guarantees and with no interaction with the outside environment or access to additional data.
### Pseudonymity
- property of a system that supports the ability of a user to use system resources or services without revealing her identity, yet with an accountability for this use
	- PO Box, user-selected ID
### Unlinkability (Nespojitelnost)
- Ensures that a user may make multiple uses of resources or services without others being able to link these uses together.
### Unobservability (Nepozorovatelnost)
- Ensures that a user may use a resource or service without others, especially third parties, being able to observe that the resource or service is being used.
	- Some methods of protection against traffic analysis (later)
## How to measure/evaluate privacy (protection)?
### Two major perspectives/directions
- Mixes - systems for message transfer, usually with dummy messages, non-optimal redirections and routes
- Common Criteria - huge standard for security evaluation of IT systems and products, enables a better comparison of systems and specification of desired functionality

- TOE: Target of Evaluation – entire (evaluated) system
- TSF: TOE Security Functions – HW, SW, FW used by TOE
- TSC: TSF Scope of Control – interactions under the TOE security policy
### Unobservability
- ensures that a user may use a resource or service without others, especially third parties, being able to observe that the resource or service is being used. Levels:
	- Unobservability requires that users and/or subjects cannot determine whether an operation is being performed.
	- Allocation of information impacting unobservability, requires that the TSF provide specific mechanisms to avoid the concentration of privacy related information within the TOE. Such concentrations might impact unobservability if a security compromise occurs.

	- Unobservability without soliciting information, requires that the TSF does not try to obtain privacy related information that might be used to compromise unobservability.

	- Authorized user observability, requires the TSF to provide one or more authorized users with a capability to observe the usage of resources and/or services.
### Unlinkability
- ensures that a user may make multiple uses of resources or services without others being able to link these uses together.
- 1 level:
	- requires that users and/or subjects are unable to determine whether the same user caused certain specific operations.
### Anonymity
- ensures that a user may use a resource or service without disclosing the user's identity. The requirements for Anonymity provide protection of the user identity. Anonymity is not intended to protect the subject identity.
- 2 levels:
	- Anonymity requires that other users or subjects are unable to determine the identity of a user bound to a subject or operation.
	- Anonymity without soliciting information enhances the requirements of FPR_ANO.1 Anonymity by ensuring that the TSF does not ask for the user identity.
### Pseudonymity
- ensures that a user may use a resource or service without disclosing its user identity, but can still be accountable for that use.
- 3 leves:
	- Pseudonymity requires that a set of users and/or subjects are unable to determine the identity of a user bound to a subject or operation, but that this user is still accountable for its actions.
	- Reversible pseudonymity, requires the TSF to provide a capability to determine the original user identity based on a provided alias.
	- Alias pseudonymity, requires the TSF to follow certain construction rules for the alias to the user identity.
### Common Criteria approach
- Existential view - the fce/property either is or not
- Common Criteria don't consider how the property was achieved.
- CC don't allow for another then discrete (Y/N) evaluation
- Granularity only by the specified levels

### Mixes - attacker perspective
- All statements are made from the perspective of an attacker who may be interested in monitoring what communication is occurring, what patterns of communication exist, or even in manipulating the communication. The attacker may be an outsider tapping communication lines or an insider able to participate in normal communications and controlling at least some stations.
#### Anonymity - mixes
- Anonymity of a subject means that the subject is not identifiable within a set of subjects, the **anonymity set**.
- The anonymity set is the set of all possible subjects. With respect to actors, the anonymity set consists of the subjects who might cause an action.
#### Unlinkability - mixes
- Unlinkability only has a meaning after the system in which we want to describe anonymity properties has been defined and the attacker has been characterized.
- Unlinkability of two or more **items of interest** (IOIs, e.g., subjects, messages, actions, ...) from an attacker’s perspective means that within the system (comprising these and possibly other items), the attacker cannot sufficiently distinguish whether these IOIs are related or not.
#### Unobservability
- Unobservability of an item of interest (IOI) means
	- undetectability of the IOI against all subjects uninvolved in it and 
	- anonymity of the subjects involved in the IOI even against the other subjects involved in that IOI.
- For messages in mixes it is undetectability of real messages apart from dummy traffic.
#### Pseudonym
- A pseudonym is an identifier of a subject other than one of the subject’s real names.
- Pseudonymity is the use of pseudonyms as identifiers.

## Identity
- An identity is any subset of attribute values of an individual person which sufficiently identifies this individual person within any set of persons.
- Identities may of course comprise particular attribute values like names, identifiers, digital pseudonyms, and addresses – but they don’t have to.
